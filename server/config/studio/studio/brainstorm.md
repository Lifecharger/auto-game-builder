# Game/App Brainstorm — Structured Ideation Framework

## Step 1: Concept Seed
Start with ONE of these triggers:
- **Mechanic-first**: "What if the player could [verb] [noun]?"
- **Fantasy-first**: "I want the player to feel like a [role]"
- **Market-first**: "There's a gap in [genre] for [audience]"
- **Remix**: "Take [Game A]'s mechanic + [Game B]'s theme"

## Step 2: Core Questions
Answer these before writing any code:
1. What does the player DO every 30 seconds? (core verb)
2. What does the player DECIDE every 2 minutes? (strategic choice)
3. What makes the player COME BACK tomorrow? (retention hook)
4. How does the player SHOW OFF? (social/achievement)
5. Where does MONEY come from? (monetization — even if free, plan it)

## Step 3: Scope Check
For a solo dev / small team with AI automation:
- Target 2-4 weeks to first playable build
- Maximum 5 core screens (menu, gameplay, shop, settings, results)
- Maximum 3 interconnected systems (more = exponential complexity)
- Art style must be achievable with AI generation (pixel art, simple 2D, UI-focused)
- Sound must be achievable with AI generation (ElevenLabs, generated SFX)

## Step 4: Differentiation
Your game competes with 1000+ others. What's YOUR angle?
- Unique mechanic combination
- Underserved audience
- Superior polish in a specific area
- Novel theme or aesthetic
- Better monetization ethics (no predatory mechanics)

## Step 5: Output Format
Generate a complete GDD following the 8-section standard:
1. Overview, 2. Player Fantasy, 3. Core Loop, 4. Detailed Mechanics,
5. Formulas & Data, 6. Edge Cases, 7. Dependencies, 8. Monetization & Retention

Then generate an initial tasklist.json with 5-10 micro-tasks to scaffold the project.
